About

About Me

I am Jiangyue Zeng, an undergraduate student at Peking University. My academic interests lie at the intersection of generative AI, computer vision, and creative media. I am particularly interested in AIGC, generative models, diffusion models, image and video editing, controllable generation, and AI-assisted visual creation.

My current interests include training-free controllable generation and video editing or relighting methods that preserve structure, temporal consistency, and visual details. I am exploring how generative models can become more controllable, interpretable, and useful for creative workflows.

Research

Research Interests

I am interested in methods that make visual generation more controllable, consistent, and useful for both research and creative practice.

Generative AI & AIGC

Studying models and workflows for generating, editing, and composing visual content with flexible user control.

Diffusion Models

Exploring diffusion-based image and video synthesis, including attention control, inversion, and guidance strategies.

Controllable Image and Video Generation

Designing mechanisms that improve layout, identity, motion, and semantic control without heavy retraining.

Video Editing and Relighting

Investigating video-level editing methods that preserve temporal coherence, geometry, and fine-grained visual detail.

AI for Creative Media

Applying generative models to creative visual tasks, including art, design, pattern generation, and media production.

Multimodal Understanding and Generation

Connecting visual, textual, spatial, and temporal signals for more expressive generation and reasoning.
